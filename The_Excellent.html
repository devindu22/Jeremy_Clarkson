Discover the Excellence of Legendary Handcraftmanship

The Excellent was a luxury car made by Jeremy Clarkson in attempts to make a 'true luxury SUV' in one of the episode of The Grand Tour (s01e09). Clarkson had the chassis and engine of a Land Rover Discovery and the body of a Mercedes SL bolted together. The original The Excellent was fitted with a chassis of a Land Rover Discovery, with the body of a 1978 MG MGB, and used a 3.9-liter V8, that was named the MGD.

About Jeremy Clarkson

He is best known for the motoring programmes Top Gear and The Grand Tour alongside Richard Hammond and James May. He also currently writes weekly columns for The Sunday Times and The Sun. Clarkson hosts the ITV game show Who Wants to Be a Millionaire?, and co-hosts the reality show Clarkson's Farm.

The Grand Tour

The Grand Tour is a British motoring television series, created by Jeremy Clarkson, Richard Hammond, James May, and Andy Wilman, made for Amazon exclusively for its online streaming service Amazon Prime Video, and premiered on 18 November 2016. The programme was conceived in the wake of the departure of Clarkson, Hammond, May, and Wilman from the BBC series Top Gear and was originally contracted with 36 episodes over three years.

Making an Impression

Jeremy Clarkson gathered James May and Richard Hammond. Initially, the two were annoyed by Clarkson's creation, which they stated was rubbish. To prove them wrong, Clarkson drove along on the road. On the road, his co-presenters noticed several design flaws on the interior of the Excellent which was unconvincing. Clarkson then decided to attract football players to get a positive reaction. After leaving the football park area, Clarkson stated he would sell the Excellent for £14,000. As Clarkson was determined to sell it at that value, the Excellent was set to auction. Initially, it was retailing under £400, but as it raised in value, it was sold for £4,000, which was underappreciated. However, in the first series of Clarkson Farm (s01e06), you can see that Clarkson kept the Excellent as one of his genius modifications.

Furthermore

Consequently, the MGD lacked proper features used in a car, such as speedometers or brakes. Additionally, the MGD's body would slip off the chassis at times. Clarkson decided to stop using the MGD after its failure to stay attached to the chassis. He then began development of The Excellent after purchasing a 4.4-litre twin-turbocharged V8, which he fitted in an old Mercedes G-Wagen. Clarkson kept the luxurious features found on the Mercedes G-Wagen and had the body modified to fit his custom-built chassis. The Excellent was then tested on the Top Gear test track where it received positive reviews from both his co-presenters and the audience. Despite some minor issues with handling, the Excellent was hailed as a significant improvement over the MGD.
